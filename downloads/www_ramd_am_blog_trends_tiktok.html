Tiktok Trends - April 2026

Introducing "TikTok Trends 2026" – your source for staying ahead of the game on the latest TikTok crazes!

TikTok trends move fast, and if you're not quick, you'll miss out. But let's be real, keeping up with TikTok trends can be a hassle. That's where we come in.
Each week, we'll keep you updated on what's hot on TikTok (by spending way too much time scrolling). From trending hashtags to popular sounds and formats, we've got you covered!

Please note:

  • Our focus here is TikTok, but we also track Meta trends on our Instagram Trends page, updated weekly. Testing trends on both platforms helps you see what really resonates.
  • Trends are generally very short, which may concern you about not mentioning the product enough. However, the mistake would be to tack on promotional elements after the video. It's crucial that the video stays true to the trend, lasts only a few seconds, and captures the essence of the need your service addresses in a brief timeframe.
  • Not all trends will be suitable for every brand. It will be up to you to choose the ones that seem most relevant to your message and goals.

27 April 2026

The “Can you see me” trend

This trend taps into that painfully relatable feeling of being ignored. Using the “can you see me” audio, creators highlight moments where they’re clearly there… but somehow completely overlooked.

It’s a mix of funny and slightly embarrassing, built around those small situations where you’re trying to get attention and it’s just not happening.

The more specific and real it feels, the better it works.

How to do the trend

  • Use the “can you see me” audio
  • Film a simple talking clip or reaction
  • Add text overlay describing your situation
  • Keep it short and very specific
  • Lean into slightly embarrassing or relatable moments

Scenario examples:

  • me telling my friend to use xxx to organize their life but they keep using random notes
  • me telling them to follow a structured plan instead of guessing every workout

The tone should feel like:“I literally gave you the solution… why are you still struggling?”

20 April 2026

The “Top 5 horrors” trend

This trend takes a simple list format and flips it into something funny and slightly unhinged. It starts like a normal “top 5” ranking, but instead of actual items, creators list things that feel like a horror movie in real life.

It’s minimal, text-driven, and highly relatable. The humor comes from how random, personal, or oddly specific the “horrors” are.

How to do the trend

  • Use a static image (selfie, random photo, or aesthetic background)
  • Add text: “top 5 horror movies”
  • List 5 items that aren’t movies but feel stressful, awkward, or scary
  • Make the list feel personal or specific

Scenario examples:

Turn the list into a mix of relatable “horrors,” where one of them is life before using the product or app. Example for a productivity app:

  1. 8am meetings
  2. my screen time report
  3. life before xxx when i planned everything in my notes app
  4. group projects
  5. emails that start with “just circling back”

14 April 2026

The “This, not that” trend

This trend shows how the same phrase can have two completely different meanings. Creators highlight the version they actually relate to versus the version people usually assume.

It often starts with a line like “trying to explain I mean this, not that,” followed by two contrasting visuals or ideas. The humor comes from clarifying the reality behind a lifestyle, habit, or identity.

How to do the trend

  • Start with text like: “trying to explain I mean this, not that”
  • Show two contrasting versions of the same idea
  • Use visuals, text, or emojis to clearly separate both sides

Emojis can help hold attention, but text-only versions work too.

Scenario examples:

Show that the improvement comes from using the right tool, not just more effort.

  • not constantly busy vs having systems that simplify tasks
  • not restricting everything vs managing money more clearly
  • not working more hours vs working more efficiently with the app

Highlight the smarter approach by placing the app name or logo on the “this” side, showing that the tool helps achieve better results without extra stress.

7 April 2026

The “Loving life again” trend

This trend captures the feeling of things finally falling back into place. Using the lyric “and just like that I’m back to loving life again,” creators show the moment life feels lighter, calmer, or more enjoyable again.

It often reflects a small reset, a glow-up, or simply removing something that was making life harder. The tone is soft, positive, and reflective, making it perfect for showing progress or a return to balance.

How to do the trend

  • Use the “loving life again” audio
  • Film a simple talking clip or aesthetic b-roll
  • Add text overlay explaining what changed or improved
  • Show a small win, improvement, or positive shift

Works as a talking video, daily life clips, or simple lifestyle footage

Scenario examples:

Show how life improved after solving a problem with the product or app.

  • “loving life again because dating feels easier”
  • “loving life again because I feel consistent again”
  • “loving life again because everything is finally organized”
  • “loving life again because I finally understand my spending”

The idea is to connect the feeling of relief or progress to the role the product played in making things easier.

The “Self-aware” trend

This trend uses the Self Aware audio as a backdrop for reflective, cinematic content. Creators pair aesthetic visuals with text overlays that feel thoughtful, emotional, or slightly introspective.

The format is flexible: it can feel calm, confident, deep, or quietly empowering. The combination of beautiful visuals and meaningful text makes the content feel personal and memorable.

How to do the trend

  • Use the Self Aware audio
  • Use aesthetic clips (sunset, city walk, coffee moment, daily life scenes)
  • Add text that shares a realization, mindset shift, or perspective

Works well as short b-roll sequences or calm selfie-style clips

How can creators use it:

Use the sound as a backdrop while showing how life feels easier, calmer, or more enjoyable thanks to the product or app.

  • Productivity:
    showing a more peaceful routine or organized day
    text overlay about feeling more in control
  • Finance:
    showing everyday moments without money stress
    text overlay about clarity or confidence
  • Dating:
    showing relaxed lifestyle moments
    text overlay about being more intentional
  • Health / wellness:
    showing balanced daily habits
    text overlay about consistency or feeling better
