How to Find Songs from TikTok Videos: Every Reliable Method

February 1, 2026 by Samuel Waihenya

You’re watching a TikTok video and suddenly a catchy song plays that you can’t get out of your head. The problem is you don’t know the song name or who sings it. This happens all the time because TikTok videos often use trending music, original sounds, or remixed tracks that aren’t easy to identify.

The fastest way to find songs from TikTok videos is to check the bottom of the video screen where TikTok displays the song title and artist name, but if that shows “Original Audio” or doesn’t help, you can use music identification apps like Shazam or paste the video link into specialized tools such as TrackSniff. These methods work whether you’re on your phone or computer.

You have multiple options to track down any song you hear on TikTok. Some methods work better than others depending on whether the song is popular, how it’s mixed in the video, and what device you’re using. Learning the right approach saves you time and helps you build your music playlist quickly.

Key Takeaways

  • Check the bottom of TikTok videos first for song information before trying other methods
  • Use music recognition apps or specialized song finder tools like TrackSniff when the song isn’t labeled clearly
  • Multiple free tools exist to identify songs from TikTok including Google Assistant and lyric search features

Best Ways to Identify Songs from TikTok Videos

TikTok videos often feature catchy music that gets stuck in your head, but finding the exact song can be tricky. You can identify TikTok sounds through built-in app features, specialized music discovery apps, or by searching lyrics directly within the platform.

1. Check the TikTok Sound Info and Audio Clip

TikTok displays song information at the bottom of most videos. You can tap on the sound name to view the full track details, including the artist and song title.

This takes you to a dedicated page showing all videos using that audio clip.

When a song comes from TikTok’s music library, you’ll see options to save it or open the track in streaming services like Spotify and Apple Music. The sound page also shows how many videos use that particular audio.

However, if the video shows “Original Sound” instead of a song title, the creator may have recorded their own audio or edited existing music. In these cases, TikTok won’t provide automatic song details. You’ll need to use other methods to identify the track.

2. TrackSniff Bespoke Recognition Tool with URL Import

TrackSniff offers a direct approach for finding songs on TikTok. You simply paste the TikTok video link into the tool, and it analyzes the audio to identify the music. This eliminates the need to play videos through separate apps.

The tool works particularly well for viral TikTok songs that might be remixed or sped up. You don’t need to download any other software. Simply create an account and start finding songs you love. The system processes the audio clip directly from the URL you provide.

This method saves time when you’re browsing TikTok on your computer. You can quickly copy links from multiple videos and identify several songs in a row without switching between apps.

3. Use Music Discovery and Song Identification Apps

Shazam stands out as the most reliable music identification app for TikTok sounds. On iPhone, Shazam integrates directly into the Control Center, so you can identify songs without leaving TikTok. Android users can enable split-screen mode to run Shazam and TikTok simultaneously.

SoundHound provides similar functionality when Shazam doesn’t recognize a track. You’ll need to play the TikTok video on one device while running SoundHound on another. The app works with both Apple Music and Spotify for easy listening.

Musixmatch and Genius focus specifically on lyrics-based identification. These apps help when you remember words from a viral sound but don’t know the song title. Both services maintain large databases of song lyrics.

4. Search Lyrics or Keywords within TikTok

TikTok’s Discover tab lets you search for songs using partial lyrics. Tap the magnifying glass icon and type any words you remember from the audio clip.

Switch to the “Sounds” section in the search results to see matching tracks.

This method works well for finding songs that have become viral sounds. Many users add the chorus or catchy phrases to their video captions, making lyrics easier to find. You can also search for keywords related to the song’s theme or genre.

The Discover feature shows you how popular each sound is based on video count. This helps you verify you’ve found the right track, especially when multiple songs share similar lyrics.

Explore Popular Streaming Service Playlists

Spotify and Apple Music regularly update playlists featuring trending TikTok songs. Search for terms like “TikTok Viral” or “TikTok Hits” within your preferred streaming service. These curated collections include both new releases and older tracks that have gained popularity on the platform.

Many playlist creators organize songs by month or genre. This makes it easier to find that specific viral sound you heard recently. You can also follow these playlists to stay updated on trending music.

Some streaming services create weekly playlists that track the most-used TikTok sounds. These update automatically as new songs go viral, giving you a reliable way to discover popular tracks without searching individually.

Tips and Extra Tools for TikTok Song Discovery

Beyond the basic methods built into TikTok, several third-party tools and creative techniques can help you track down elusive songs. Voice recognition technology and community resources offer additional paths when standard identification methods fall short.

Make Use of Third-Party Song Finder Websites

Several music discovery apps and websites specialize in identifying TikTok audio. Musixmatch stands out for its massive lyrics database, letting you search by typing any words you remember from the song. The platform works well when you caught part of the lyrics but need the full track details.

Genius offers similar lyrics-based search capabilities. You can enter fragments of what you heard and browse through results until you find the right match.

AHA Music works as a browser extension that identifies songs playing in any tab.

This tool proves useful when browsing TikTok on your computer rather than your phone.

These websites complement the more popular apps like Shazam and SoundHound by offering different search methods.

Utilize Voice Search and Hum Recognition

Google’s voice search includes powerful music identification features that work when you can’t get clear audio.

Open the Google app and tap the microphone icon, then select “Search a song.” You can hum, whistle, or sing the melody even if you don’t know the words.

The technology compares your humming to its database and returns matching songs. This method works surprisingly well even with off-key humming.

YouTube Music also includes hum recognition. The feature lets you search by sound rather than requiring the exact audio from the TikTok video.

This proves valuable when the TikTok audio includes talking or sound effects over the music.

For iPhone users, Siri handles voice search requests. Say “Hey Siri, what song is this?” while a TikTok video plays. The system uses built-in Shazam technology to identify tracks and can add them directly to Apple Music playlists.

Ask TikTok Creators and Check Hashtags

Sometimes the direct approach works best. Comment on the TikTok video asking about the song name. Many creators respond to these questions, especially on smaller accounts where engagement matters.

Check the video description for hashtags related to the music. Creators often tag songs with #songname or #artistname even when using original audio. Look for hashtags that seem music-related rather than content-related.

The TikTok Discover tab lets you search these hashtags. Type what you see and filter by Sounds to find the audio track. This method works particularly well for remixes or lesser-known versions that traditional apps like Shazam might miss.

You can also visit Spotify and search for “TikTok Viral” or “TikTok Hits” playlists. These curated collections feature trending songs from the platform and get updated regularly.
